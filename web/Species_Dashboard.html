Countries with >100 Critically Endangered, Endangered, or Vulnerable Species



To the left, we have a bar chart showing countries and the number of critically endangered, endangered, or vulnerable species that this country is home to. For the purpose of simplicity, we have only graphed countries which have 100 or more at-risk species. Please note that there are a total of 238 countries that currently have at-risk species. The chart to the right shows the top 44 countries and their count of species.


As we can see, it seems the countries whose wildlife is being affected the most are:

  • Indonesia with 535 at-risk species
  • Columbia with 495 at-risk species
  • Mexico with 417 at-risk species
  • Ecuador with 411 at-risk species
  • Brazil with 389 at-risk species
  • India with 360 at-risk species
  • China with 360 at-risk species

Species Populations from 1996-2021


To the right, we have a line chart tracking the population of each type of species from the years 1996 to 2021. As shown in the legend in the top left corner of the chart, each line corresponds to the population count for birds, mammals, and reptiles with a separateline charting the total population count for all three species types combined. Please refer to the legend for reference.

The default view of this chart is showing the population count for all species currently within the "Critically Endangered" category. Use the dropdown above to show species population counts for species in the "Endangered" and "Vulnerable" categories!

As we can see, it seems the population for all animal type in the "Critically Endangered" category has increased over time suggesting that although these species are still critically endangered, conservation efforts have made it possible for these species to grow.
For "Endangered" species, we see a similar upward trend where endangered birds, mammals, and reptiles have successfully grown in numbers although still remain endangered. It is important to note that much of this overall growth may be due to the sudden increase in endangered reptile populations; as the population count for mammals and birds has remained pretty steady.
Species in the "Vulnerable" caegory also have an upward trend overall however, in this category we can see that vulnerable mammal populations have took a dip in 2008 and have no been able to recover since. While ir populations seem to remain steady, it seems that reptile populatiions are once again the biggest contribution to the overall uptick in population counts for vulnerable species.

Conservation Efforts



To the left, we have a donut chart displaying the most prevalent conservation efforts currently being used to combat many of the threats that critically endangered, endangered, and vulnerable species are facing. Each of these efforts are shown with the percentage describing how often this conservation method is used in relation to our database of species.


Please note that any conservation effort which was utilized 2% or less was placed into the "Other" category for readability purposes. As a reminder, for the purposes of our project, our database contains the following types of species: birds, mammals, and reptiles.


Below are brief descriptions of the most popular conservation efforts that can be observed in our donut chart:

  • Site/are protection: Protecting the geographical site where the species lives through legal means. These protected sites are designated by national authorities as scientific reserves with limited public access, national parks, natural monuments, nature reserves or wildlife sanctuaries, protected landscapes, and areas managed mainly for sustainable use.
  • Site/area management: Management of a protected area that require regular, active interventions to address the requirements of particular species within the area. For example, protecting vegetation patterns or other biological features,protecting fragments of habitats as components of landscape or seascape-scale conservation strategies
  • Resource and habitat protection: Focus on protecting the habitats in which the at-risk species live in. This promotes sustainable use of natural resources and social and economic benefits to local communities where relevant.
  • Awareness & communications: To develop public education and appreciation of the species and/or habitats concerned. Communication, Education and Public Awareness are important instruments for conservation and sustainable use of biodiversity. Motivates people towards mobilizing individual and collective action.
